Concept Art

Definition

Concept art involves creating character ideas, movements, and environments before going into production (Shamsudim, Islam, and Islam 2013). The primary goal is to generate a visual demonstration, give an idea of the artist's imagination, and communicate between thought and visualization (Enea, 2023).

Mary Blair, who was a concept artist at Disney, describes her job as collaborating with writers and helping to generate ideas from their basic beginning in the graphically correct form (Canemaker 2014).

Mary Blair

In the world of graphic design, there was a female design pioneer who left a significant impact on the graphic design field until this day. Her name is Mary Blair. She was a concept artist and an influential designer for Disney Animation classics such as Cinderella, Alice in Wonderland, Peter Pan, and Dumbo (Holt 2019).

Her signature was vibrant colour and modern shape (Adams 2017). She worked in the animation field, which was men-dominated, yet her impact and voice were incredible.

Disney Ride

Mary Blair's genre changed in the 1940s to be more fantasy, capturing the imagination. Her designs are solid, modern, childlike, and flat, with beautiful, uncommon colour combinations and shapes (Canemaker 2014). Her design was ahead of time, predating the other artists Stuart Davis, Milton Avery, and Adolf Gottlieb(Male 2019).

Mary Blair was influenced by the cubism work (Catamaran 2014) by Pablo Picasso, which had flat colour blocks instead of generating a deep illusion of work (Tate Britain 2024). Mary Blair also used vibrant colours and fantastic, odd abstract colours for her illustrations (Adams 2017), which is similar to Vincent Van Gogh, who utilized unconventional colours (MoMa, n.d.), vibrant colours, and high contrast for his modern paintings (MoMa, n.d.).
