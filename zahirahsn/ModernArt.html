Modern Art

Vincent Van Gogh

Vincent van Gogh is a Dutch artist born on March 30, 1852 (van Gogh-Bonger and Gayford 2015). His fantastic artwork, Starry Night, depicted the world based on his feelings, sentiments, and moods. And become the most famous modern painter (Avdeeva and Degyarenki 2020).

Van Gogh struggled with the night sky, capturing the environment by exploring and implementing the elements of the landscape of ST Paul, the insane asylum, such as the village, trees, and mountain (The Museum of Modern Art 2015)

Pablo Picasso

Pablo Picasso was born on October 25 1881, in Malaga, Spain. His existence brought so much development. In modern art, he developed a new style called cubism (Walther 2000).

The style emphasizes flat, two-dimensional and geometric forms. He has the ability to combine sources (Tate.org.uk, n.d.). His famous art, Guernica, depicted the political statement and protest symbol of Naziz's bombing practice during the Spanish Civil War (Werner, n.d.).

Salvador Dali

He is the most influential modern art artist. Salvador was born on May 11, 1904, in a small town in Catalan, northern Spain (Shanes 2010). The Persistence of Memory, 1931, is one of his famous artworks for modern art.

The surrealist style becomes the symbol of his paintings. Ant represents decay and paints himself as the fleshy creature with cliffs from Dali's home (Gromley 2023).
