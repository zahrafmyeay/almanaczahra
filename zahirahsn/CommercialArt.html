Commercial Art

Children Book

"I Can Fly", published in 1950, was written by Ruth Krauss and illustrated by Mary Blair. Mary Blair has a signature style called "Mary Blair Children". The character has a simple round face and red cheeks with flat-style shapes (Brooks 2015).

This book was awarded by the Children's Spring Book Festival and was successful in commercials for sixty years (Canemaker 2014).

Advertising

In the late 1950s and early 1960s, Mary Blair had commercial projects in her home studio in Great Neck, New York (Brooks 2015).

She designed the advertising for various brands such as MeadowGold, Kraft Foods, Pall Mall, Dutch Boy Paints, Hanes underwear, Blue Bell Clothing, and Johnson & Johnson (Holt 2019).

Disney Ride

Small World Ride was a boat ride with the theme of United Nations Children. It envisions celebrating children around the world. Mary Blair designed characters from all around the world, and the ride burst with her art style.

She played with colour and shape and brought the diversity of culture and landscape to children. The ride became the most popular attraction in Walt Disney. (Holt 2019)
