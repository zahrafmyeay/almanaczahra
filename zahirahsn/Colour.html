Vibrant Complimentary

The Starry Night

Van Gogh used a new painting style with light and unblended colours to depict that he was a modern artist. Van Gogh uses vibrant colours, high contrast, and high impact complementary colour theory from Delacraux's painting (Van Gogh Museum, n.d.).

In the starry night, Van Gogh use yellow for the moon, the greenish blue of the sky, the royal blue water and the mauve ground. It depicted the view from the asylum window that combine his imagination and astronomical aspect (Thomson and Museum of Modern Art 2008). Vincent uses colours that are different and unconventional. (MoMa, n.d.)

Crisp silouhettes and Bright Colours

The persistence of Memory

The colour of Deli's art was influenced by Picasso, especially in the crisp silhouettes and the bright colours (Shanes 2010). He has surrealist practicality and creates an illusion of a dream through the object and the colours shown without the brush texture. He has a signature colour, which is yellow from Naples (Kritsky, Mader, and Smith 2013).
