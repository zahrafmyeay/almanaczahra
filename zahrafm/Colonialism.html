Colonialism

Definition of Colonialism

Colonialism is a practice of occupation and domination to expand the territory, enrich the coloniser, and involve violence and dispossession. The artwork beside is one of the colonialism definitions of Dutch through the Dutch East India Company or VOC. (Magdoff et al., 2001).

Furthermore, Colonialism refers to artwork that emerged around the period of European colonization. Furthermore, Colonial art get the influenced and reliance on European subject matter and its art movement (tingatinga, 2023).

Concise History

Colonialism began in 1800 when the Industrial Revolution flourished in Britain and continued developing in other European regions (Hoffman, 2015, 3).

The reasons behind this vary, such as economic exploitation, natural resources, or the coloniser population's expansion (Magdoff et al., 2001).

The Industrial Revolution was a tool that opened the way for Europeans to take over and reach 35% of the earth's land (Hoffman, 2015).

The colonialism practice is still happening these days, attested by Zionism as a coloniser in Palestine and genocide of the native population.

Colonialism in Painting

The Arrest of The Prince Diponegoro
Raden Saleh
1854

The painting expresses the disagreement with the painting by Pieneman, "The Submission of Prince Dipo Negoro to General De Kock". He depicted the actual situation by taking reference to a diary kept by the Dutch military commander, eyewitnesses, and the relevant passages about Diponegoro that were written by his closest confidantes of Prince Diponegoro, Raden Adipati Danureja V Yogyakarta (P. B. R. Carey, 1982)

In this painting, Saleh conveys nationalism through the historical painting as a precaution against rebellion since Saleh himself experienced Colonialism as the Dutch arrested his relative during the Java War (P. Carey, 2020, 10; Krauss, 2005, 288).

He followed the Romanticism style, which he learned during the colonisation era and greatly influenced him to higher his status among Europeans (Fauzie 2019, 220).
