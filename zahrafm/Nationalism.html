Nationalism

Definition of Nationalism

Nationalism is an ideology and a devotion of individuals to the nation-state. It can be seen as an attitude or an action to achieve the sustain and self-determination. Nationalism includes the sense of involvement within the community and the patriotism and political doctrine of individuals (Miscevic, 2001).

The American and French revolutions were the first powerful manifestations of nationalism, and it emerged during the 18th century and then continued to spread its wings to other Europe following that century. The American and French revolution depicted in painting by John Trumbull that portray the surrendering to French and American army (Siege of Yorktown) in 1781. (Kohn, 2024).

Nationalism in paintings

Through physical action, symbolisation, and metaphor, painting can be considered nationalism, such as presenting a flag fabric or uniform. Nationalism influences the artist's judgement of critique, which usually reflects society (Laugée & Rabiller, 2020).

Nationalism can be found in several paintings, such as Raden Saleh's The Arrest of Prince Diponegoro and Washington Crossing Delaware, which both paintings represent the national history scene. At the same time, William Barak depicts the culture as a pride of the Aboriginal and First Nation and crafts the traditional values of its country.

Corroboree

William Barak
1895

William Barak is an Indigenous Australian artist. His painting represents indigenous culture and resistance to the colonial era by emphasising nationalism: the cultural identity as central to the survival and preservation of history (Ryan AM, 2014).

The painting "Corroboree" portrays the ceremonial gathering or corroboree from an Aboriginal perspective as a viewer. Moreover, it is a very essential drawing by William Barak. He drew that to record a culture encountering destruction by the white invasion. He was one of the few people with firsthand knowledge about the indigenous traditional language, culture, song and religious law (Ryan AM, 2014).
